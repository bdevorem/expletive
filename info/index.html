About the Project

How It All Started


xpl3t!v3 started out as a course project for Dr. David Chiang's Theory of Computing course at the University of Notre Dame, but has turned into so much more. Inspired by random chat in the #lug Slack channel (under the Notre Dame- Computer Science and Engineering team, Breanna and Nick both hold admin positions in NDLUG) about esolangs, programming paradigms, and strange Turing-completeness, we wanted to create a language that seemed unreadable to the untrained eye, but a Turing-complete language nonetheless. This led to the search for Turing-complete computational models and Post's 2-tag system, where xpl3t!v3 was born. After that, Breanna and Nick fell in love with their Turing-complete and preciously expletive language and have decided to continue work. Check back here for updates.

The Theory Behind It All


How can we do all of this? Because the 2-Tag system is Turing-complete! The reason that a 2-tag system (or any system with more than one tag) can simulate any Turing machine comes from the fact that the queue can significantly grow or shrink at any point, by either a reduction of two or one, no change in length, or an addition of varying length, based on the rules defined (http://mathworld.wolfram.com/TagSystem.html).

Developers


The developers of xpl3t!v3 are two bright-eyed and eagerly passionate undergraduate Computer Science students at the University of Notre Dame, Breanna Devore-McDonald and Nicholas C Jones. Check out their webpages for more details.
